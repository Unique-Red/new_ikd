Bola Ahmed Tinubu

Asiwaju Bola Ahmed Adekunle Tinubu is a Nigerian politician and a national leader of the All Progressives Congress.He was the Executive Lagos State Governor from 1999 to 2007.

Tinubu was born on 29 March 1952 in Osun State, Nigeria. His mother, Abibatu Mogaji, was a trader that later became the Iyaloja of Lagos.

He attended St. John's Primary School, Aroloya, Lagos and Children's Home School in Ibadan, South West of Nigeria. Tinubu then went to the United States in 1975, where he studied first at Richard J. Daley College in Chicago, Illinois, and then at Chicago State University. He graduated in 1979 with a Bachelor of Science degree in Accounting.

Tinubu worked for the American companies Arthur Andersen, Deloitte, Haskins, & Sells, and GTE Services Corporation. After returning to Nigeria in 1983, Bola Tinubu joined Mobil Oil Nigeria, and later became an executive of the company.

His political career began in 1992, when he joined the Social Democratic Party where he was a member of the Peoples Front faction led by Shehu Musa Yar'Adua and made up of other politicians such as Umaru Yar'Adua, Atiku Abubakar, Baba Gana Kingibe, Rabiu Kwankwaso, Abdullahi Aliyu Sumaila, Magaji Abdullahi, Dapo Sarumi and Yomi Edu. He was elected to the Senate, representing the Lagos West constituency in the short-lived Nigerian Third Republic.

After the results of the 12 June 1993 presidential elections were annulled, Tinubu became a founding member of the pro-democracy National Democratic Coalition, a group which mobilized support for the restoration of democracy and recognition of Moshood Abiola as winner of the 12 June election. Following the seizure of power as military head of state of General Sani Abacha, he went into exile in 1994 and returned to the country in 1998 after the death of the military dictator, which ushered in the transition to the Fourth Nigerian Republic.

In the run-up to the 1999 elections, Bola Tinubu was a protégé of Alliance for Democracy (AD) leaders Abraham Adesanya and Ayo Adebanjo.He went on to win the AD primaries for the Lagos State governorship elections in defeating Funsho Williams and Wahab Dosunmu, a former Minister of Works and Housing.

In January 1999, he stood for the position of Governor of Lagos State on the AD ticket and was elected governor.

Tinubu is a Muslim. He is married to Oluremi Tinubu, the current senator of the Lagos Central senatorial district.His nephew, Adewale Tinubu is the CEO of Oando.


Prince Abiodun Ogunleye

Prince Abiodun Ogunleye is a leading political figure in Ikorodu. A former deputy Governor of Lagos State, a member of the Governor’s Advisory Committee and a rare breed politician.

Prince Abiodun Ogunleye was born 1st of November, 1940 into the Petu Branch of the Rademo ruling house of Ikorodu, Lagos state. He had his elementary education at the Holy Trinity School, Ebute Ero, Lagos. Thereafter, he studied at the Methodist Boys high school, Lagos before his quest for further education took him to the United Kingdom in 1966 where he read Accountancy at the North West polytechnic Kentish Town, London and the West Bronwich college of Commerce and Technology Wednesbury, England. He sharpened his skill and knowledge with management courses both in England and Japan before returning to Nigeria.

Popularly called DG by friends and political associates has a robust working career that revolves around accounting and auditing. As a young secondary school leaver, he was employed by the Federal Audit Department as an audit clerk in 1962. While studying in the U.K, he worked in the Accounts and Audit Department of London Borough of Brent from 1966 to 1969 and as an Accountant with R.K. Burt & Co Limited in London from 1969 to 1971. Between November 1973 and May 1974, he worked with Wickman Serivener Limited at Aston, Birmingham in England. Armed with a professional qualification in Accountancy, baba Ogunleye returned home to pick up a job as an accountant with the Lagos State Ministry of Finance in July 1974.

He left the Ministry to begin a career in the private sector not knowing he would return to the same ministry 21 years later as a Commissioner. Baba Ogunleye served in various capacities in the private sector notably as an accountant at Galvanizing Industries Limited where he rose through the rungs of the ladder to become the Executive Director, Finance thus becoming the first Nigerian Executive Director and the most senior Nigerian at the time in the company. He went on to become the Honourable Commissioner for Health and social welfare in 1994 and Honourable Commissioner Finance and Economic Development in 1995. He was appointed by Asiwaju Bola Ahmed Tinubu in 2003 as the Director of the Lagos Metropolitan Area Transport Authority (LAMATA) before he was later picked as the Deputy Governor of Lagos state May 2007.

DG is a member of the Institute of Chartered Secretaries and administrators (ICSAN), institute of Chartered Accountants of Nigeria (ICAN) and the institute of Administrative Management of Nigeria.

He is a good family man with children of both sexes and a very notable football lover.
