Prince Abiodun Ogunleye

Prince Abiodun Ogunleye is a leading political figure in Ikorodu. A former deputy Governor of Lagos State, a member of the Governorâ€™s Advisory Committee and a rare breed politician.

Prince Abiodun Ogunleye was born 1st of November, 1940 into the Petu Branch of the Rademo ruling house of Ikorodu, Lagos state. He had his elementary education at the Holy Trinity School, Ebute Ero, Lagos. Thereafter, he studied at the Methodist Boys high school, Lagos before his quest for further education took him to the United Kingdom in 1966 where he read Accountancy at the North West polytechnic Kentish Town, London and the West Bronwich college of Commerce and Technology Wednesbury, England. He sharpened his skill and knowledge with management courses both in England and Japan before returning to Nigeria.

Popularly called DG by friends and political associates has a robust working career that revolves around accounting and auditing. As a young secondary school leaver, he was employed by the Federal Audit Department as an audit clerk in 1962. While studying in the U.K, he worked in the Accounts and Audit Department of London Borough of Brent from 1966 to 1969 and as an Accountant with R.K. Burt & Co Limited in London from 1969 to 1971. Between November 1973 and May 1974, he worked with Wickman Serivener Limited at Aston, Birmingham in England. Armed with a professional qualification in Accountancy, baba Ogunleye returned home to pick up a job as an accountant with the Lagos State Ministry of Finance in July 1974.

He left the Ministry to begin a career in the private sector not knowing he would return to the same ministry 21 years later as a Commissioner. Baba Ogunleye served in various capacities in the private sector notably as an accountant at Galvanizing Industries Limited where he rose through the rungs of the ladder to become the Executive Director, Finance thus becoming the first Nigerian Executive Director and the most senior Nigerian at the time in the company. He went on to become the Honourable Commissioner for Health and social welfare in 1994 and Honourable Commissioner Finance and Economic Development in 1995. He was appointed by Asiwaju Bola Ahmed Tinubu in 2003 as the Director of the Lagos Metropolitan Area Transport Authority (LAMATA) before he was later picked as the Deputy Governor of Lagos state May 2007.

DG is a member of the Institute of Chartered Secretaries and administrators (ICSAN), institute of Chartered Accountants of Nigeria (ICAN) and the institute of Administrative Management of Nigeria.

He is a good family man with children of both sexes and a very notable football lover.
